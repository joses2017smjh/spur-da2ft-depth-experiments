\section{Problem and Setup}

Given a stereo pair of PRO depth maps (and optionally RGB images and camera
poses), the models produce \textbf{refined metric-scale depth maps} per view.
The key challenges being addressed are:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item \textbf{Scale ambiguity} --- monocular PRO depth has an arbitrary per-image scale
    \item \textbf{View inconsistency} --- left and right views produce inconsistent depth
    \item \textbf{Edge noise} --- depth boundaries are noisy at thin structures
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Common Interface}

Both models share the same forward signature and output contract:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{Input} & \textbf{Shape} & \textbf{Required?} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
\texttt{rgb}    & $(B,\;V,\;3,\;H,\;W)$ & \texttt{use\_rgb=True} & RGB images, ImageNet-normalised \\
\texttt{d\_pro} & $(B,\;V,\;1,\;H,\;W)$ & Always                 & PRO monocular depth, median-scaled \\
\texttt{pose\_T}& $(B,\;V,\;4,\;4)$     & \texttt{use\_pose=True} & World-to-camera transforms \\
\midrule
\texttt{output} & $(B,\;V,\;1,\;H,\;W)$ & ---                    & Refined metric depth per view \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Here $V = 2 \cdot n\_\text{pairs}$ is the total number of views; for a single
stereo pair $V=2$ (left $L$ and right $R$).  The default training resolution is
$H=280$, $W=512$.

\section{\textcolor{flaggray}{Configuration Flags}}

Both models accept the following flags that ablate or remove specific inputs:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lllp{6.5cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Flag} & \textbf{Default} & \textbf{Applies to} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
\texttt{n\_views}   & 2    & Both  & Total views to fuse; 2 = single stereo pair, 6 = 3 pairs \\
\texttt{use\_rgb}   & True & MVP   & False $\Rightarrow$ depth-only encoder ($\text{in\_ch}=1$, no RGB concatenation); not supported in DINO (ViT-L requires RGB) \\
\texttt{use\_pose}  & True & Both  & False $\Rightarrow$ no pose embedding at bottleneck; views are fused purely from appearance \\
\texttt{no\_fusion} & False & Both & True $\Rightarrow$ each view decoded independently --- disables both pose and cross-view fusion (strongest ablation) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Current target configuration.}
Single stereo depth pair, no RGB, no pose:
\begin{verbatim}
# MVP
python train_mvp_stereo.py --no_rgb --no_pose ...
# DINO  (RGB required by ViT; pose disabled)
python train_mvp_stereo_dino.py --no_pose ...
\end{verbatim}

\section{\textcolor{cnnblue}{MVP Model: MVStereoUNet (CNN baseline)}}

\subsection{Overview}

A from-scratch UNet where all views share encoder and decoder weights.
Cross-view information is exchanged at the bottleneck via a $1\times1$
convolution that sees all views simultaneously.

\subsection{Per-View Encoder}

The encoder accepts a concatenated input per view and produces a bottleneck
feature map plus four skip connections.

\[
x_i =
\begin{cases}
[\,\text{rgb}_i \;\|\; \text{d\_pro}_i\,] \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times 4 \times H \times W} & \text{use\_rgb=True} \\
\text{d\_pro}_i \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times 1 \times H \times W} & \text{use\_rgb=False}
\end{cases}
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Operation} & \textbf{Output shape} & \textbf{Role} \\
\midrule
\texttt{enc0} & \texttt{ConvBlock}($c_\text{in} \to 64$)          & $(B,\;64,\;H,\;W)$        & Skip $s_0$ \\
\texttt{enc1} & \texttt{MaxPool + ConvBlock}($64 \to 128$)        & $(B,\;128,\;H/2,\;W/2)$   & Skip $s_1$ \\
\texttt{enc2} & \texttt{MaxPool + ConvBlock}($128 \to 256$)       & $(B,\;256,\;H/4,\;W/4)$   & Skip $s_2$ \\
\texttt{enc3} & \texttt{MaxPool + ConvBlock}($256 \to 512$)       & $(B,\;512,\;H/8,\;W/8)$   & Skip $s_3$ \\
\texttt{bnk}  & \texttt{MaxPool + ConvBlock}($512 \to 512$)       & $(B,\;512,\;H/16,\;W/16)$ & Bottleneck $b_i$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Where $c_\text{in} = 4$ (\texttt{use\_rgb=True}) or $1$ (\texttt{use\_rgb=False}).
Each \texttt{ConvBlock} is: Conv$3\times3 \to$ BN $\to$ ReLU $\to$ Conv$3\times3 \to$ BN $\to$ ReLU.

\subsection{Pose Injection (optional, \texttt{use\_pose=True})}

Camera geometry is encoded as three absolute scalars relative to a reference
camera (\texttt{box/shot01}):

\[
\mathbf{e}_i = \begin{bmatrix}
T[2,3] - 0.85 \\
\text{atan2}(R[1,0],\,R[0,0]) + 3.0885 \;\;(\text{wrapped to } [-\pi,\pi]) \\
\sqrt{T[0,3]^2 + T[1,3]^2} - 12.148
\end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^3
\]

These encode height above ground, azimuth angle, and radial distance from the
trunk respectively.  They are projected to a $p_\text{ch}$-dim embedding and
broadcast to the bottleneck spatial grid:

\[
\mathbf{p}_i = \text{PoseProject}(\mathbf{e}_i) \in \mathbb{R}^{p_\text{ch}}, \quad
\tilde{b}_i = [\,b_i \;\|\; \mathbf{p}_i\,] \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times (512 + p_\text{ch}) \times h \times w}
\]

where $p_\text{ch} = 32$ by default, $h = H/16$, $w = W/16$.

When \texttt{use\_pose=False}, $\tilde{b}_i = b_i$ (no pose appended).

\subsection{Cross-View Fusion}

All $V$ pose-augmented (or plain) bottlenecks are concatenated and reduced
to a single shared bottleneck via a learned $1\times1$ convolution:

\[
b_\text{fused} = \text{Conv}_{1\times1}\!\bigl([\,\tilde{b}_0 \;\|\; \cdots \;\|\; \tilde{b}_{V-1}\,]\bigr),
\quad
\underbrace{V \times (512 + p_\text{ch})}_{\text{or } V \times 512} \;\to\; 512
\]

This single step allows the network to transfer depth cues across views --- if
the trunk is occluded in one view the other can fill in the information.

\subsection{Per-View Decoder}

Each view is decoded using the \textbf{same fused bottleneck} but its
\textbf{own} view-specific skip connections:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
\textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Operation} & \textbf{Output shape} & \textbf{Skip} \\
\midrule
\texttt{up3} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($1024 \to 512$) & $(B,\;512,\;H/8,\;W/8)$  & $s_3$ \\
\texttt{up2} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($768 \to 256$)  & $(B,\;256,\;H/4,\;W/4)$  & $s_2$ \\
\texttt{up1} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($384 \to 128$)  & $(B,\;128,\;H/2,\;W/2)$  & $s_1$ \\
\texttt{up0} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($192 \to 64$)   & $(B,\;64,\;H,\;W)$        & $s_0$ \\
\texttt{head}& Conv$1\times1(64 \to 1)$ + \texttt{softplus}        & $(B,\;1,\;H,\;W)$         & --- \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\texttt{softplus} guarantees positive depth:
$\text{softplus}(x) = \ln(1 + e^x)$.

\subsection{Parameter Count}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
\textbf{Configuration} & \textbf{Parameters} & \textbf{Notes} \\
\midrule
\texttt{use\_rgb=True, use\_pose=True}  & $\approx 31$M & Full model \\
\texttt{use\_rgb=False, use\_pose=True} & $\approx 30$M & Depth-only encoder \\
\texttt{use\_rgb=False, use\_pose=False}& $\approx 30$M & Depth-only, no pose \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{\textcolor{dinogreen}{DINO Model: MVStereoDINOUNet}}

\subsection{Overview}

A drop-in replacement for \texttt{MVStereoUNet} that swaps the from-scratch CNN
encoder for a \textbf{frozen DINOv2 ViT-L backbone} (304M params) combined with
a trainable \textbf{Depth Side Branch}.  The pose encoding, cross-view fusion,
and decoder are structurally identical.  Only $\approx 5$M parameters are
trained.

\subsection{DINOv2ViTLEncoder}

DINOv2 requires 3-channel RGB and cannot accept a 4-channel concatenation.
Depth is therefore processed in a \emph{parallel} branch and fused at the
skip-connection level.

\subsubsection{RGB path (frozen)}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item Pad RGB to nearest multiple of 14: $W=512 \to 518$, $H=280 \to 280$
  \item Pass through DINOv2 ViT-L/14 (frozen)
  \item Extract intermediate token outputs from blocks 5, 11, 17, 23
  \item Reshape tokens $(B, N, 1024) \to (B, 1024, h_t, w_t)$ where $h_t = \lceil H/14 \rceil$
  \item Project $1024 \to 256$ (skips) and $1024 \to 512$ (bottleneck) via $1\times1$ conv
  \item Upsample to target skip resolutions
\end{enumerate}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lllll}
\toprule
\textbf{Block} & \textbf{Token shape} & \textbf{After reshape} & \textbf{Projected} & \textbf{Upsampled to} \\
\midrule
5  & $(B,N,1024)$ & $(B,1024,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,H,W)$       = $s_0$ \\
11 & $(B,N,1024)$ & $(B,1024,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,H/2,W/2)$   = $s_1$ \\
17 & $(B,N,1024)$ & $(B,1024,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,H/4,W/4)$   = $s_2$ \\
23 & $(B,N,1024)$ & $(B,1024,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,256,H/8,W/8)$   = $s_3$ \\
23 & $(B,N,1024)$ & $(B,1024,h_t,w_t)$ & $(B,512,h_t,w_t)$ & (kept at $H/14$)    = $b$   \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsubsection{Depth path (trainable)}

The Depth Side Branch is a three-stage strided CNN:

\[
\text{d\_pro} \xrightarrow{\text{Conv}_{3\times3}\,\text{stride-1}} \mathbf{d}_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{B\times32\times H\times W}
\xrightarrow{\text{stride-2}} \mathbf{d}_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{B\times64\times H/2\times W/2}
\xrightarrow{\text{stride-2}} \mathbf{d}_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{B\times128\times H/4\times W/4}
\]

\subsubsection{Skip fusion}

ViT skips and depth features are fused via concatenation and $1\times1$ conv:

\[
s_k^{\text{fused}} = \text{Conv}_{1\times1}\!\bigl([s_k^{\text{ViT}} \;\|\; \mathbf{d}_k]\bigr)
\in \mathbb{R}^{B \times 256 \times \cdot \times \cdot}, \quad k \in \{0,1,2\}
\]

Skips $s_3$ and bottleneck $b$ are at resolutions too coarse for depth detail
($H/8$ and $H/14$), so no depth fusion is applied there.

\subsection{Pose Injection and Cross-View Fusion}

Identical to \texttt{MVStereoUNet}: absolute 3-scalar pose encoding $\to$
\texttt{PoseProject} $\to$ broadcast concat at bottleneck $\to$
$1\times1$ fusion conv.  Controlled by \texttt{use\_pose} flag.

\subsection{DINODecoder}

Uniform 256-channel skips require a different decoder width schedule compared
to the CNN decoder:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Operation} & \textbf{Output shape} \\
\midrule
\texttt{up3} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($512 + 256 \to 256$) & $(B,\;256,\;H/8,\;W/8)$ \\
\texttt{up2} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($256 + 256 \to 256$) & $(B,\;256,\;H/4,\;W/4)$ \\
\texttt{up1} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($256 + 256 \to 128$) & $(B,\;128,\;H/2,\;W/2)$ \\
\texttt{up0} & Upsample + cat + \texttt{ConvBlock}($128 + 256 \to 64$)  & $(B,\;64,\;H,\;W)$ \\
\texttt{head}& Conv$1\times1(64 \to 1)$ + \texttt{softplus}             & $(B,\;1,\;H,\;W)$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Parameter Count}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrl}
\toprule
\textbf{Component} & \textbf{Parameters} & \textbf{Trainable?} \\
\midrule
DINOv2 ViT-L backbone         & $\approx 304$M & \textcolor{warnred}{Frozen} \\
Skip projection ($1\times1$ convs) & $\approx 3.0$M & Yes \\
Depth side branch              & $\approx 0.1$M & Yes \\

Pose MLP + fuse conv           & $\approx 0.3$M & Yes \\
DINODecoder                    & $\approx 1.8$M & Yes \\
\midrule
\textbf{Total}     & $\approx 310$M & \\
\textbf{Trainable} & $\approx 5.7$M & \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Loss Functions}

\subsection{Primary Loss: Scale-Invariant Log (SiLog)}

All depth pixels are gated by a trunk mask \emph{and} a depth validity range
$[d_\text{min},\, d_\text{max}]$ (default $[0.5\,\text{m},\; 10.0\,\text{m}]$).
Background seen through canopy gaps has true Blender depth of $77$--$200\,$m
and must be excluded even though it falls inside the silhouette mask.

For each view $v$, letting $\hat{d}$ and $d$ denote predicted and GT depth:

\[
m^{(v)}_{ij} = \text{mask}^{(v)}_{ij}
               \cdot \mathbf{1}[d^{(v)}_{ij} > d_\text{min}]
               \cdot \mathbf{1}[d^{(v)}_{ij} < d_\text{max}]
\]

\[
\delta^{(v)}_{ij} = \bigl(\ln \hat{d}^{(v)}_{ij} - \ln d^{(v)}_{ij}\bigr) \cdot m^{(v)}_{ij}
\]

\[
n_v = \sum_{i,j} m^{(v)}_{ij} + \varepsilon
\]

\[
\mathcal{L}_{\text{silog}}^{(v)}
= \sqrt{\max\!\left(
    \frac{\sum_{i,j} (\delta^{(v)}_{ij})^2}{n_v}
    \;-\;
    \alpha \cdot \left(\frac{\sum_{i,j} \delta^{(v)}_{ij}}{n_v}\right)^{\!2},\;
    0
  \right)}
\]

where $\alpha = 0.85$ (variance focus).  The total supervised loss is:

\[
\mathcal{L}_{\text{silog}} = \sum_{v=0}^{V-1} \mathcal{L}_{\text{silog}}^{(v)}
\]

\paragraph{Why SiLog?}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Scale-invariant}: predicting $c \cdot \hat{d}$ for all pixels
        gives $\delta_{ij} = \ln c$ (constant), so the variance term is zero and
        a uniform scale error is fully absorbed.
  \item \textbf{Structure-focused}: the $\alpha$ term down-weights global mean
        error in favour of local depth variation --- the model learns the
        \emph{shape} of the trunk surface, not just its absolute distance.
  \item \textbf{Robust}: log-space errors are bounded even for very large or
        very small predicted values, unlike MSE.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Optional: Stereo Multi-View Consistency Loss}

When \texttt{--lambda\_mv $> 0$}, a geometric consistency term is added after a
warmup period (\texttt{--mv\_warmup\_epochs}).  For each stereo pair $(L, R)$,
a \emph{bidirectional} warp is computed:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item Unproject source GT depth to 3D using intrinsics $K$:
        $\mathbf{P}_\text{src} = K^{-1}[u,v,1]^\top \cdot d_\text{GT,src}$
  \item Transform to target frame: $\mathbf{P}_\text{tgt} = T_{s\to t}\, \mathbf{P}_\text{src}$
  \item Project onto target image: $[u_t, v_t] = K\,\mathbf{P}_\text{tgt} \,/\, z_t$
  \item Sample predicted depth at $(u_t, v_t)$: $\hat{d}_\text{tgt}^{\text{warped}}$
  \item Loss: $\bigl|\ln \hat{d}_\text{tgt}^{\text{warped}} - \ln z_t\bigr|$ on valid pixels
\end{enumerate}

Only stereo pairs with baseline $\leq 0.80\,$m contribute.  Valid pixels must
be in-bounds, trunk-masked in both views, and depth-gated.  The total loss is:

\[
\mathcal{L}_\text{total}
= \mathcal{L}_\text{silog} + \lambda_\text{mv} \cdot \mathcal{L}_\text{warp}
\]

GT depth is used for unprojection (not predictions) so the geometric signal is
reliable even when model predictions are poor early in training.

\subsection{Evaluation Metric: Masked RMSE}

\[
\text{RMSE}^{(v)} = \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i,j} m^{(v)}_{ij}\,(\hat{d}_{ij}^{(v)} - d_{ij}^{(v)})^2}{\sum_{i,j} m^{(v)}_{ij} + \varepsilon}}
\]

averaged over all $V$ views.  Units are the same as depth (Blender units
$\approx$ metres in this dataset).

\section{MVP vs DINO Comparison}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lp{4.5cm}p{4.5cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Component} & \textbf{MVP (MVStereoUNet)} & \textbf{DINO (MVStereoDINOUNet)} \\
\midrule
Encoder & From-scratch UNet CNN & Frozen DINOv2 ViT-L/14 \\
Depth handling & Concatenated with RGB at input (or depth-only) & Separate trainable depth branch, fused at skip level \\
Skip channels & 64 / 128 / 256 / 512 (progressive) & Uniform 256 at all 4 levels \\
Bottleneck channels & 512 & 512 (projected from ViT 1024) \\
Trainable params & $\approx 31$M (all) & $\approx 5.7$M (backbone frozen) \\
Pose encoding & Identical & Identical \\
Cross-view fusion & Identical $1\times1$ conv & Identical $1\times1$ conv \\
Output activation & \texttt{softplus} & \texttt{softplus} \\
Loss functions & SiLog + optional warp & Identical \\
\texttt{use\_rgb=False} & Supported (in\_ch=1) & Not supported (ViT requires RGB) \\
\texttt{use\_pose=False} & Supported & Supported \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
